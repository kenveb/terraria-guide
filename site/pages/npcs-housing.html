NPC Housing & Happiness Guide

NPCs in Terraria need houses to move in. Once they're housed, you can manage their happiness by pairing them with neighbors they like in biomes they enjoy. Happy NPCs give you discounts and sell pylons for fast travel.

Valid House Requirements

A house must meet these minimum requirements to be considered valid:

  • Interior size of at least 60 tiles (e.g., 10 wide x 6 tall)
  • Interior size of no more than 750 tiles
  • Background walls —must be player-placed. Natural dirt or stone won't work
  • A valid light source (torch, candle, chandelier, etc.)
  • A valid flat-surface item (workbench, table, etc.)
  • A valid comfort item (chair, throne, bed, etc.)
  • A valid door (or trap door or platforms acting as entrance)
  • The house must not be in a corrupt/crimson biome

Quick House Template

The classic "prison" style: 10 wide x 6 tall with wood walls, one torch, one workbench, one chair, and a wooden door. This qualifies as a valid house and takes minimal materials.

NPC Happiness —How It Works

Each NPC has preferences for where they want to live (biome) and who they want to live with (neighbors). Meeting these preferences increases their happiness, which:

  • Lowers prices (up to 75% of normal at max happiness)
  • Increases sell prices for items you sell to them
  • Unlocks the Pylon for that biome at high enough happiness

Crowding Penalty

Having more than 3 NPCs within 120 blocks of each other gives a happiness penalty. Spread out your NPCs across different biomes. Don't build a giant NPC tower in one spot.

Pylon Network

Once you have a pylon, you can teleport between any two pylons from anywhere on the map. Build one pylon per biome for fast travel across your entire world. This is essential for Hardmode when you're traversing long distances.
